ThunderClan


ThunderClan is a primarily peaceful and respectful Clan that fiercely displays courage, loyalty, and ferocity during battles.

While they have a reputation for welcoming and providing shelter to wandering loners and kittypets, this practice became more common only after Firestar's leadership. Some other Clans perceive this as a sign of weakness.

ThunderClan's typical prey includes mice, voles, squirrels, rabbits, starlings, magpies, wood pigeons, blackbirds, and thrushes, and their stalking techniques are highly skilled.

They maintain upwind positions when approaching prey in dense bushes. ThunderClan felines are notably robust and are the only ones to flex their hindquarters before leaping toward their prey.

Riverclan


RiverClan is among the five Clans that hold StarClan in high regard as their warrior ancestors.

Formerly residing in a treeless, reedy, and pebbly expanse beside a river, they now occupy a territory encompassed by two streams.

The felines of RiverClan possess unparalleled skills in fishing and a fondness for water.

They also appreciate the beauty and collect rocks, shells, and feathers to adorn their dens.

Their diet primarily consists of fish, but they also consume water voles, shrews, and mice. RiverClan cats are contented, well-fed, and have groomed coats.

While they are perceived as stubborn and indolent by outsiders, they are known to be clever and graceful.

Their lengthy and lustrous fur aids them while swimming. During leaf-bare, RiverClan sustains itself by fattening up on fish during greenleaf.

They dislike adding birds to their fresh-kill pile, and other Clans sometimes derisively refer to them as "fish faces" or "fish breath."

RiverClan felines are proficient swimmers, moving through the water with ease, and possess lightning-fast reflexes while catching fish.

They remain well-nourished even in leaf-bare, and their sleek and attractive pelts often appeal to Twolegs.

Some Twolegs even take them in as pets, although RiverClan keeps it discreet, as not all such cats return to the Clan.

WindClan


WindClan cats have a reputation for loving open spaces, as they live on the moors and catch hares and rabbits as their main prey.

They are smaller and wirier than cats from other Clans. Unlike other Clans, they dislike wet ground and prefer sleeping on the grassy moorland under the open sky.

Despite their loyalty and

toughness, they can be nervous and quick to flee due to a lack of cover on the moors.

WindClan takes pride in being the closest Clan to the Moonstone, where their leaders and medicine cats communicate with StarClan.

They are considered the quickest and most quick-witted of the Clans but are also the easiest to offend and the least likely to start battles.

WindClan cats' pelts are usually shades of gray and brown, providing natural camouflage on the moors.

They have the most knowledge of Twolegs, as they frequently encounter them on the farms surrounding their territory.

ShadowClan


ShadowClan is a proud and defensive Clan that is often seen as arrogant and aggressive by other Clans.

Despite their reputation for being battle-hungry and greedy for territory, they are misunderstood.

In fact, they have the smallest amount of territory and prey, which may justify their willingness to add anything to the fresh-kill pile.

Although ShadowClan's fierce warriors and hostile leaders have contributed to their reputation, their leader Dawnstar believes that they were not always as unfair and cruel as they seem now.

Their main prey is reptiles and amphibians, and they are known to hunt rats when prey is scarce.

They are skilled at hunting at night and are adept at sneaking through the undergrowth. ShadowClan cats also use shadows to their advantage in battle.

In the old forest, their secret food source was the carrion place, although they were careful not to eat carrion.

SkyClan


SkyClan, the fifth Clan, was forced to leave their forest territory due to its destruction, which was later occupied by Twolegplace cats.

Their leader at the time, Cloudstar, pleaded with the other Clan leaders at a Gathering, asking for them to give up some of their lands for SkyClan. However, all the Clans refused, and SkyClan was eventually driven out.

As a result, Cloudstar vowed that SkyClan would never again seek guidance from their warrior ancestors.

Cloudstar's mate, Birdflight, was offered a place in ThunderClan with her young kits, as they were too vulnerable to make the journey to SkyClan's new home.

Birdflight accepted the offer with much regret and promised to reunite with Cloudstar someday. In Firestar's Quest, the ThunderClan leader, Firestar, and his mate, Sandstorm, successfully bring back SkyClan.

Modern SkyClan cats are characterized by their sturdy hind legs inherited from their ancestors, the ancient SkyClan.

They have rough, durable gray pads that allow them to jump and traverse long distances on hard, rough surfaces without injuring themselves, making them ideal for climbing and hunting in trees, thus earning them their name.

They are formidable hunters, much like ThunderClan, and primarily prey on squirrels, mice, voles, and various bird species.

StarClan


StarClan is a group of deceased ancestors, descendants, and Clanmates of the cats in the Clans.

To join StarClan after death, a cat must have lived an honorable life and followed the warrior code.

Those who didn't follow the code ended up in the Place of No Stars or the Dark Forest.

A cat doesn't have to be in a Clan to join StarClan; kittypets, rogues, and loners can also be accepted.

When a Clan cat dies, they are guided to StarClan by a deceased cat who played an important role in their life.

StarClan cats are recognized by their starry paw prints, shining eyes, and glowing pelts. Their fur carries the scents of the seasons.

StarClan cats remain in the group until they are forgotten by all living and dead cats, after which they fade away to ghost-like images.

Although StarClan cats can be killed again, they vanish forever to a different place.

Leaders who lose a life appear in StarClan as a faint copy but cannot communicate until they lose their final life.
